Hi, I'm Yennie
I am a writer, world traveler, and technologist seeking to share my understanding of the world.
Previously a full-stack software engineer on the Education team at Microsoft, I am currently based in Seoul, South Korea.
***

I am part of the Big Data Studies Lab at Seoul National University, where I participate in several different projects at the intersection of data science and computational history.

One of my current research areas is looking at civil service officials from the Korean Joseon Dynasty (1392-1897) across different sources to find missing people and trace geneaologies.

I am also interested in the future of historical and archival research, especially in the age of Big Data - what "historical artifacts" will our society today leave for historians of the future?
